StreamKit User Manual

Your all-in-one real-time communication platform — chat, video calls, group meetings, and live streaming in one place.

01 Welcome to StreamKit

StreamKit is Rajutechie's unified real-time communication platform. Whether you're exchanging quick messages with a teammate, hopping on a video call, hosting a company-wide meeting, or broadcasting a live stream to thousands of viewers, StreamKit brings all of it under one roof — no plugins, no downloads required for viewers.

👥

🎙️

RTMP broadcast to unlimited viewers with live chat interaction
🖥️

🔔

02 Setting Up Your Account

Getting started with StreamKit takes about two minutes. Follow the steps below to create your account and configure your device permissions.

Account Creation Steps

  1. 1
    Open the app URL provided by your administrator (e.g., https://stream.yourorg.com) in a supported browser.
  2. 2
    Enter your display name and email address on the registration screen. Your display name is what other users will see in chats and calls.
  3. 3
    Confirm your email address by clicking the verification link sent to your inbox. Check your spam folder if it doesn't arrive within 2 minutes.
  4. 4
    Grant camera and microphone permissions when prompted by your browser. These are required for video calls and meetings. You can grant them later in your browser settings.
  5. 5
    Upload a profile avatar (optional) — a square image (JPG or PNG, max 2 MB). You can always do this later from your Profile settings.
💡

You can sign in with an existing account token if your admin provides one — simply paste the token on the login screen instead of registering a new account. This is useful for single-sign-on (SSO) environments.

Account Fields Reference

FieldRequired?Description
displayNameYesThe name shown to other users in chats, calls, and meetings. Can be changed at any time in Profile settings.
emailYesUsed for account verification, email notifications, and account recovery. Must be a valid email address.
avatarUrlNoURL or uploaded image for your profile picture. If omitted, StreamKit generates an initial-based avatar automatically.
roleNoAssigned by your administrator. Determines access levels: user (default), moderator, or admin.

Device Permissions Explained

📷
Camera
Required for video calls and meetings. You can disable it per-call using the camera toggle button.
🎤
Microphone
Required for audio in calls and meetings. You can mute yourself at any time during a call.
🔔
Notifications
Allows StreamKit to send browser push notifications for incoming calls and messages, even when the tab is in the background.

04 Chat & Channels

Channels are the core unit of conversation in StreamKit. Think of them as named group chat rooms — organized around teams, topics, or projects.

Channel Types

🌐
Public Channels
Visible and joinable by all users on the platform without an invitation
🔒
Private Channels
Invite-only channels. Only members can see the channel and its messages
👁️
Unlisted Channels
Not shown in the public directory, but joinable via a direct link

Finding and Joining a Channel

Click the Chat icon in the left sidebar, then click Browse Channels. Use the search box to find a channel by name or keyword. Click Join next to any public channel to become a member immediately.

Creating a New Channel

  1. 1
    Click the + New Channel button at the top of the channel list in the Chat sidebar.
  2. 2
    Enter a channel name (lowercase letters, numbers, and hyphens only, max 50 characters).
  3. 3
    Add an optional description so other users know what the channel is for.
  4. 4
    Select the visibility: Public, Private, or Unlisted.
  5. 5
    Click Create Channel. You are automatically made the channel owner.

Member Roles

RolePermissions
OwnerFull control — edit settings, invite or remove members, delete the channel, pin messages
MemberSend messages, upload files, react, reply to threads, view message history

Channel Settings Reference

visibilitypublic / private / unlistedControls who can discover and join the channel.
chatEnabledtrue / falseIf false, the message composer is disabled and the channel becomes read-only. Useful for announcement channels.
ℹ️

Channel capacity: Channels support unlimited members. Message retention: By default, message history is retained indefinitely. Your administrator may configure a retention policy (e.g., 90 days) — contact them for details about your organization's data policy.

05 Sending Messages

The message composer is at the bottom of every channel and direct message view. It supports rich text, file attachments, and user mentions.

Composing and Sending a Message

Click the message input field, type your message, and press Enter to send. To add a newline without sending, press Shift+Enter.

Attaching Files and Images

Click the paperclip icon (or drag and drop a file into the message input area) to attach files. Supported formats: images (JPG, PNG, GIF, WebP), documents (PDF, DOCX, XLSX, TXT), and videos (MP4, WebM). Maximum file size is 50 MB per attachment.

🖼️ [ Screenshot: Message composer with attachment button, emoji picker, and send button highlighted ]

Mentioning a User

Type @ followed by the user's display name to trigger the mention autocomplete. Select the user from the dropdown and they will receive a notification. Use @everyone to notify all channel members (moderators and owners only).

Slash Commands

CommandDescription
/me <text>Sends an action message (e.g., "Alice is away")
/clearClears the message composer (does not delete sent messages)
/invite @userInvites a user to the current private channel (owner only)
/leaveLeaves the current channel

Editing and Deleting Your Messages

Hover over any of your own messages to reveal the action toolbar. Click the pencil icon to edit the message in place, or click the trash icon to delete it. Deleted messages are replaced with "This message was deleted" for other users who saw it.

ℹ️

Message character limit: Each message can contain up to 4,000 characters. For longer content, consider uploading a text file as an attachment.

06 Reactions & Threads

StreamKit makes it easy to engage with messages without cluttering the chat — use emoji reactions, threaded replies, and pinned messages to keep conversations organized.

Adding Emoji Reactions

Hover over any message to reveal its action toolbar, then click the smiley face icon to open the emoji reaction picker. Select an emoji to add your reaction. Click the same emoji again to remove it. Reactions are displayed as grouped counters below the message — click any reaction to see who reacted.

💡

Long-press on mobile to open message options, including the emoji reaction picker, reply, edit, and delete actions.

Thread Replies

To keep a focused conversation without interrupting the main channel flow, use threads. Hover over a message and click Reply in Thread. A thread panel slides open on the right where you can continue the conversation. Thread replies show a preview in the main channel so others can follow along.

🖼️ [ Screenshot: Thread panel open on the right side, showing replies to a parent message ]

Read Receipts

In direct messages and small channels (fewer than 20 members), you'll see checkmark indicators below your messages showing who has read them. A single checkmark means the message was delivered; a double checkmark (shown with member avatars) means it has been read by those users.

Pinning Messages

Channel owners and moderators can pin important messages so they're easy to find later. Hover over a message, click the three-dot menu, and select Pin Message. Pinned messages are visible by clicking the pin icon at the top of the channel. Up to 10 messages can be pinned per channel.

07 One-on-One Video Calls

StreamKit lets you start a high-quality video or audio call with any user directly from a chat or their profile page — no scheduling required.

⚠️

Make sure you've allowed camera and microphone permissions in your browser before starting a call. If you haven't, your browser will prompt you when the call begins. You can also pre-grant permissions in your browser's site settings.

Starting a Call

  1. 1
    Open a direct message with the user, or click their profile avatar anywhere in the app.
  2. 2
    Click the video camera icon for a video call, or the phone icon for an audio-only call.
  3. 3
    StreamKit will ring the other user. Wait for them to accept. You can cancel the outgoing call at any time by clicking End Call.
  4. 4
    Once connected, the call window opens. Use the controls at the bottom to manage audio, video, and more.

Accepting or Rejecting an Incoming Call

When someone calls you, a notification banner appears at the top of the screen (and as a browser push notification if the tab is in the background). Click Accept (green button) to join the call or Decline (red button) to reject it. Declining sends a "missed call" notification to the caller.

In-Call Controls

ControlIconShortcutDescription
Mute / Unmute🎤MToggle your microphone on and off
Camera On / Off📷VEnable or disable your video feed
Share Screen🖥️SShare your screen, window, or browser tab
Hang Up📵EscEnd the call for both participants
Switch Camera🔄—Toggle between front and rear camera (mobile only)
Full Screen⛶FExpand the call to fill the entire screen

Call Quality Indicators

A small signal-strength icon in the corner of each participant's video tile shows connection quality: green (excellent), yellow (fair), red (poor). If you see persistent red indicators, try closing other browser tabs or moving closer to your Wi-Fi router.

Call Duration and History

An elapsed timer at the top of the call window shows how long the call has been running. After the call ends, a summary (duration, participants, timestamp) is logged in the Calls section of the sidebar and in your direct message thread with that user.

Scheduling a Meeting

  1. 1

09 Screen Sharing & In-Call Controls

Screen sharing lets you present your work, walk through documents, or demonstrate software to call and meeting participants in real time.

⚠️

Screen sharing requires Chrome or Edge. Firefox supports window sharing only (not individual browser tabs or full-screen sharing). Safari does not support screen sharing at this time.

How to Share Your Screen

  1. 1
    During a call or meeting, click the Share Screen button (monitor icon) in the control bar, or press S.
  2. 2
    Your browser will show a picker dialog. Choose to share your entire screen, a specific application window, or a browser tab.
  3. 3
    Click Share in the browser dialog. Other participants immediately see your shared content.
  4. 4
    A red indicator appears in the control bar showing that your screen is being shared. Click Stop Sharing or click the indicator to end the screen share.

Presenter Mode

When you begin sharing your screen, StreamKit automatically switches the remote participants' layout to Presenter Mode — your screen share fills most of the view and other video tiles shrink to a strip at the bottom. You can always switch back to the Gallery view using the layout toggle in the top-right of the call window.

Picture-in-Picture Mode

Click the Picture-in-Picture button (the small floating window icon) in the call controls to detach the video call into a small overlay window. This lets you continue using StreamKit chat or browse other apps while keeping an eye on participants. Click the PiP window to return to the full call view.

Stopping Screen Share

To stop sharing, either click Stop Sharing in the StreamKit control bar, or click the "Stop sharing" button in your browser's built-in screen share notification bar at the bottom of the screen. Both methods immediately stop the screen share for all participants.

10 Watching Live Streams

StreamKit delivers live video streams using HLS (HTTP Live Streaming) with adaptive bitrate, meaning the video quality automatically adjusts to your internet connection speed.

Browsing Live Streams

Click the Live icon in the left sidebar. The stream browser shows all currently active public streams as cards — each displaying the stream title, host name, viewer count, and a thumbnail. Streams are sorted by viewer count by default.

🖼️ [ Screenshot: Live streams browser showing stream cards with thumbnails, viewer counts, and host avatars ]

Joining a Stream as a Viewer

Click any stream card to open the stream player. The video begins loading immediately — no account login is required to watch public streams if your admin has enabled anonymous viewing. The stream plays in your browser with no plugin required.

Live Chat During a Stream

While watching, a live chat panel is displayed alongside the video player. Type your message and press Enter to participate in the stream conversation. The host and other viewers can see your messages in real time. You can @mention the host or other viewers in the stream chat.

Viewer Count

A live viewer count is displayed at the top of the stream player and updates every 10 seconds. The host can also see this count from their streaming dashboard.

Adaptive Bitrate Playback

StreamKit's HLS player automatically selects the best video quality (resolution and bitrate) for your current network speed. You can also manually select a quality level using the gear icon in the player controls: Auto, 1080p, 720p, 480p, or 360p.

DVR / Rewind

If the stream host has enabled DVR mode, a timeline scrubber appears in the player. You can pause, rewind up to 60 minutes into the past, and resume watching from any point. The "LIVE" badge turns gray when you are watching a delayed portion of the stream — click it to jump back to the live edge.

💡

Streams are available directly via HLS at the URL shown in the stream info panel (click the info icon in the player). You can use this URL in VLC, QuickTime, or any HLS-compatible media player for playback outside the browser.

Reporting Inappropriate Content

If a stream violates community guidelines, click the flag icon (or three-dot menu) in the stream player and select Report Stream. Select a reason (inappropriate content, spam, harassment, copyright violation) and submit. Reports are reviewed by administrators typically within 24 hours. Reported streams may be paused pending review.

11 Going Live as a Host

StreamKit accepts RTMP (Real-Time Messaging Protocol) streams from any compatible encoder. OBS Studio (free and open-source) is the recommended tool for most hosts.

Requirements

📡
RTMP Encoder
OBS Studio (recommended), Streamlabs OBS, or any RTMP-compatible software/hardware encoder
🌐
Stable Upload Speed
Minimum 3 Mbps upload for 720p30; 6 Mbps for 1080p30. Use a wired connection when possible.
👤
Host Account
Your StreamKit account must have streaming permissions enabled by an administrator

Going Live — Step by Step

  1. 1
    In StreamKit, click the Live icon in the sidebar, then click Create Stream. Fill in the stream title, description, and visibility settings.
  2. 2
    Copy your unique RTMP Ingest URL and Stream Key from the stream dashboard. Keep these private.
  3. 3
    Open OBS Studio. Go to Settings → Stream. Set Service to "Custom…", paste the RTMP URL as "Server", and paste the Stream Key.
  4. 4
    Configure your scenes and sources in OBS (camera, capture card, game capture, etc.), then click Start Streaming in OBS.
  5. 5
    Return to StreamKit and click Go Live in your stream dashboard. Your stream will become visible to viewers within a few seconds.
⚠️

Never share your stream key publicly — treat it exactly like a password. Anyone with your stream key can broadcast to your channel. If you believe your key has been compromised, click Regenerate Stream Key in your stream settings immediately.

OBS Stream Settings

OBS Settings 
# OBS Studio → Settings → Stream
Service:     Custom...
Server:      rtmp://ingest.rajutechie-streamkit.io/live/<streamKey>
Stream Key:  <your-stream-key-from-dashboard>

# Recommended OBS Output Settings
Video Bitrate:  4500 Kbps (for 1080p30)
Audio Bitrate:  160 Kbps
Encoder:       x264 (or hardware NVENC/AMF if available)
Keyframe:      2 seconds

# HLS Playback URL for viewers
HLS URL:  https://stream.rajutechie-streamkit.io/hls/<streamKey>/index.m3u8

Ending Your Stream

Click Stop Streaming in OBS first (this stops the RTMP feed), then click End Stream in the StreamKit dashboard. This updates the stream status, archives the recording (if enabled), and notifies viewers that the stream has ended.

Viewing Your Stream Stats

While live, your stream dashboard displays real-time statistics: current viewer count, peak viewer count since going live, stream uptime, ingest bitrate, and dropped frame rate. These stats update every 5 seconds.

12 Profile & Presence Status

Your profile is how other StreamKit users identify you. Your presence status helps teammates know when you're available to chat or take a call.

Editing Your Profile

Click your avatar in the top-right corner of the app (or the Profile icon in the sidebar), then click Edit Profile. You can update your display name and profile picture at any time. Changes take effect immediately across all channels and conversations.

🖼️ [ Screenshot: Profile edit modal showing displayName field, avatar upload area, and custom status input ]

Setting Up Browser Push Notifications

  1. 1
    Go to Settings → Notifications in StreamKit.
  2. 2
    Click Enable Browser Notifications. Your browser will display a permission prompt at the top of the page.
  3. 3
    Click Allow in the browser prompt. StreamKit will confirm that notifications are now active.

Configure a time window during which all non-critical notification sounds are suppressed. Go to Settings → Notifications → Quiet Hours, set your start and end times, and enable the toggle. Incoming call notifications will still ring during Quiet Hours unless you also enable Do Not Disturb.

Mobile Push Notifications

On iOS (15+) and Android (9+), the StreamKit mobile web app registers a push notification token with your device when you first enable notifications. This token allows the server to send push alerts directly to your device even when the StreamKit tab is closed. Your push token is automatically refreshed every 30 days.

ℹ️

Email notifications are sent to the email address registered to your account. You can change your notification email (if different from your login email) in Settings → Account → Notification Email.

14 Privacy & Security

Understanding how your data is handled — and how to protect yourself — is important. This section covers visibility controls, blocking, reporting, data retention, and token security.

Who Can See Your Presence Status

By default, your presence status (Online, Away, etc.) is visible to all users who share at least one channel with you, or who are in your contacts list. You can restrict status visibility to Contacts only or Nobody in Settings → Privacy → Presence Visibility.

Blocking a User

Blocking a user prevents them from: sending you direct messages, seeing your presence status, @mentioning you, and initiating calls with you. To block someone, open their profile card (click their avatar) and select Block User. You can manage your block list in Settings → Privacy → Blocked Users.

Reporting a Message or User

To report a message, hover over it, click the three-dot menu, and select Report Message. To report a user, open their profile and click Report User. Choose a reason (harassment, spam, inappropriate content, etc.) and optionally add a note. Reports are sent to administrators and moderators for review.

Data Retention

Data TypeDefault RetentionNotes
Chat MessagesIndefiniteYour admin may configure a retention policy. Check with your admin for your org's policy.
Call Logs90 daysIncludes duration, participants, and timestamps. Does not include call recordings unless enabled.
Stream Recordings30 days (if enabled)Recordings are automatically deleted after 30 days unless the host downloads them first.
Uploaded FilesIndefiniteFiles shared in channels are stored until the channel is deleted or the admin purges old files.

Token Security

Your authentication token is like a password for your StreamKit session. Never share it with anyone, including people claiming to be administrators. Tokens expire after 24 hours by default (your admin may configure a longer or shorter duration). If you believe your token has been compromised, log out immediately, then log back in to obtain a new token.

⚠️

StreamKit does not end-to-end encrypt messages by default. Messages are encrypted in transit (TLS) and at rest, but administrators with server access can access message content. If your organization requires end-to-end encryption, contact your admin about available encryption options and compliance configurations.

Administrator-Level Controls

Platform administrators have access to additional moderation tools: banning users permanently, setting content rules (blocked words/phrases), auditing user activity logs, and configuring data retention policies. If you have concerns about how the platform is being managed, contact your organization's StreamKit administrator directly.

Detailed FAQ

📷   My camera shows a black screen or shows the wrong camera ▼

This usually happens when another application has exclusive access to your camera, or when the browser is using a different camera than expected.

Steps to fix:

  • Close any other app that might be using your camera (Zoom, Teams, FaceTime, etc.)
  • Click the camera icon in the call controls to disable and re-enable your video
  • In browser site permissions, check which camera device is selected for StreamKit
  • If you have multiple cameras, go to Settings → Audio & Video and select the correct camera device
  • Try refreshing the page and rejoining the call
🔇   Other participants can't hear me / I can't hear others ▼

Audio issues can be caused by permissions, wrong input/output devices, or mute states.

Check these things in order:

  • Verify you are not muted — the microphone icon in the control bar should not have a slash through it. Press M to toggle.
  • Check that your correct microphone and speakers are selected in Settings → Audio & Video
  • Verify browser microphone permission is granted (lock icon in address bar)
  • Check your system's audio input and output device settings
  • Try using headphones to rule out echo/feedback issues
📶   My stream is lagging or disconnecting from OBS ▼

Stream instability from OBS is almost always a network issue or a bitrate too high for your upload speed.

Steps to stabilize your stream:

  • Run a speed test and confirm you have at least twice your target bitrate in upload speed (e.g., if streaming at 4500 Kbps, you need 9 Mbps+ upload)
  • Switch to a wired Ethernet connection instead of Wi-Fi
  • In OBS, reduce the Video Bitrate to 3000 Kbps or lower and try again
  • Enable Dynamic Bitrate in OBS (Settings → Advanced → Network) to automatically adjust bitrate when connection drops
  • Check OBS's "Dropped Frames" and "Network" stats in the bottom status bar during streaming
🌐   I get "ICE failed" or call drops immediately on connect ▼

ICE (Interactive Connectivity Establishment) failures happen when WebRTC cannot establish a peer-to-peer or relay connection between participants. This is almost always a network or firewall issue.

Common causes and fixes:

  • Your corporate network or VPN may be blocking UDP traffic on ports 10000-60000. Contact your IT/admin team.
  • The StreamKit server may not have a TURN relay server configured. Ask your admin to check the TURN server configuration.
  • Try disabling your VPN temporarily to see if that resolves the issue.
  • If on a restrictive network, ask your admin to configure TURN over TCP port 443 (TLS), which bypasses most firewalls.
🔑   My login keeps failing / I'm getting "token expired" errors ▼

Auth tokens expire after a set period (default: 24 hours). If you're seeing token expiry errors repeatedly, follow these steps:

  • Click Log Out from the user menu (top-right corner)
  • Clear your browser's cookies and site data for the StreamKit domain
  • Log in again with your credentials
  • If the problem persists, ask your administrator to verify your account status — your account may have been suspended or your role may have changed
  • If your admin provides a pre-generated token, paste it directly on the login screen instead of using a password

How to Report a Bug

If you encounter an error that isn't listed above, gather information from your browser's developer console and share it with your administrator.

💡

Press F12 to open your browser's Developer Tools, then click the Console tab. Look for error messages prefixed with [SignalingServer], [mediasoup], [WebRTC], or [StreamKit]. Copy the relevant lines and include them when reporting the bug to your admin. This information significantly speeds up diagnosis.
